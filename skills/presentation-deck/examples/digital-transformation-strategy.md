# Example: Digital Transformation Strategy Briefing

## Metadata
- **Audience**: C-suite (CEO, CFO, COO)
- **Duration**: 15 minutes
- **Context**: Mid-year strategic review, investment decision gate
- **Tone**: Data-driven, confident, forward-looking

## Slide 1: Cover
**Title**: Digital Transformation: Our Path to Market Leadership
**Subtitle**: Strategic Initiative Briefing | Q2 2026 Review
**Speaker notes**: 
- Greet audience, establish credibility (past successes)
- "Today we're here to make a critical decision about our digital future"

## Slide 2: Current Situation
**Headline**: Market landscape is shifting. Our competitors are moving faster.
**Key points**:
- Customer preference for digital-first: 68% (vs. 45% last year)
- Competitors' digital revenue: +35% YoY
- Our digital revenue: +8% YoY
- Skills gap: 40% of roles require digital competency we lack

**Visual**: Line chart showing competitor velocity vs. our pace
**Speaker notes**:
- "We're falling behind. This isn't about being modern—it's about survival."
- Pause. Let that sink in.
- "Our customers are telling us they expect seamless digital experiences"

## Slide 3: Strategic Challenge
**Headline**: We need to transform. The question is how, and how fast?
**Constraints**:
- Budget: $15-25M over 3 years
- Timeline: Market window is 18-24 months
- Skills: Need to build in-house + external partnerships
- Customer expectations: No service disruption

**Visual**: Decision tree or constraint matrix
**Speaker notes**:
- "We have three options on the table"
- Outline the trade-offs (cost vs. speed vs. capability)
- "Each path has consequences"

## Slide 4: Recommended Path
**Headline**: Modernize core customer experience + build new digital-first revenue streams
**Three pillars**:
1. **Modernize Platform** (Year 1) - Migrate to cloud, API-first architecture
2. **Build Digital Products** (Year 1-2) - Launch 2-3 new digital offerings
3. **Upskill Team** (Ongoing) - 60% of team in digital roles by Year 2

**Visual**: Roadmap diagram with timeline
**Speaker notes**:
- "This positions us as digital leaders in our market"
- "It's ambitious but achievable"
- "We've seen this work at [peer company]"

## Slide 5: Investment Summary
**Headline**: Total investment: $18M | Payback period: 2.5 years | NPV: $45M
**Breakdown**:
- Platform modernization: $9M (infrastructure, tools, migration)
- New product development: $6M (people, tools, marketing)
- Team upskilling: $3M (training, hiring, consultants)

**Visual**: Waterfall chart showing phase-by-phase investment
**Speaker notes**:
- "This is 2.3% of annual revenue—a strategic bet on our future"
- "By Year 3, digital revenue will be 25%+ of total"

## Slide 6: Success Metrics
**Headline**: We'll measure success on four key metrics
- **Digital revenue**: 25% of total by 2028
- **Customer NPS**: +15 points improvement
- **Time-to-market**: New digital products in 6 months (vs. 18 months today)
- **Team capability**: 60% of team in digital-native roles

**Visual**: Gauge chart or target graphic
**Speaker notes**:
- "These are our guardrails"
- "We'll review quarterly and adjust as needed"

## Slide 7: Key Risks & Mitigation
**Headline**: We've identified risks. We have plans.
- **Risk**: Talent shortage → *Mitigation*: Partner with [consulting firm], hire early
- **Risk**: Integration challenges → *Mitigation*: Phased migration, parallel systems
- **Risk**: Market shifts → *Mitigation*: Modular approach, pivot capability

**Visual**: Risk matrix (severity vs. likelihood)
**Speaker notes**:
- "These risks are real, but manageable"
- "We've built in buffers and contingency plans"

## Slide 8: Next Steps
**Headline**: We need your decision today
- **Decision**: Approve $18M investment for digital transformation initiative
- **Timeline**: Board approval this week, kick-off June 1
- **Approval path**: CEO → Board Finance Committee → Full Board
- **If we delay**: Risk falling further behind; market window closes in 12 months

**Visual**: Stylized checkmark or "Go/No-Go" indicator
**Speaker notes**:
- "Delaying costs us time and competitive advantage"
- "Are we ready to move forward?"
- Invite questions

## Deck standards
- **Font**: Helvetica Neue or similar (sans-serif, professional)
- **Colors**: Navy blue (primary), teal (accent), white (background)
