# Skill: Strategic Screening

## Purpose
Assess an initiative, artifact, architecture, or proposal against enterprise architecture, governance, AI, data, privacy, security, and public-sector alignment criteria.

## When to use
- Reviewing an AI, data, cloud, platform, or architecture proposal
- Preparing ARB, CDO, TPO, or executive advice
- Identifying risks, gaps, misalignment, or required governance engagement
- Converting early ideas into decision-ready framing

## Inputs
- Initiative or artifact description
- Audience and decision context
- Known constraints, risks, classification, and data sensitivity
- Optional reference frameworks or policies

## Outputs
- Executive summary
- Alignment assessment
- Risk and gap register
- Required follow-up actions
- Recommendation: proceed, proceed with conditions, refine, defer, or stop

## Screening dimensions
1. Strategic alignment
2. Enterprise architecture fit
3. Data and information management fit
4. Security and privacy posture
5. AI governance and responsible AI implications
6. Operational supportability
7. Reuse and platform alignment
8. Accessibility and official-language implications where relevant
9. Cost, scalability, and sustainability
10. Decision readiness

## Workflow
1. Identify the decision being supported.
2. Extract the business capability, technology pattern, data flows, and governance implications.
3. Map the proposal to relevant policy, architecture, and platform constraints.
4. Identify blockers, risks, assumptions, and missing evidence.
5. Produce a concise recommendation with traceable rationale.

## Quality checks
- Does the assessment distinguish facts from assumptions?
- Are unresolved risks explicit?
- Are governance touchpoints named?
- Is the recommendation actionable?
- Is the language suitable for senior management?

## Default recommendation scale
- Proceed
- Proceed with conditions
- Refine and resubmit
- Defer pending evidence
- Stop / not recommended

## Notes
Use scoped, defensible language. Do not claim compliance unless evidence is provided. Prefer phrasing such as "aligned with", "requires validation", "appears consistent with", or "not yet demonstrated".
