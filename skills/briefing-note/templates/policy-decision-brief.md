# Template: Policy Decision Brief

## Use Case
Government/organizational policy decision briefing for ADM/leadership review

## Standard Structure

### Issue
1-2 sentence framing of the decision/problem
- What is the core question?
- Why does it need a decision now?
- Who is affected?

**Example:**
```
The Department must decide whether to adopt a zero-trust security architecture 
across all government IT systems. This decision will affect 50,000+ users and 
require significant investment over 3 years.
```

### Background
2-3 paragraphs providing context
- History/what got us here
- Current state assessment
- What has already been tried

**Example:**
```
The Department currently operates under a traditional perimeter security model 
where users inside the network firewall have broad access to systems and data. 
However, this model is increasingly inadequate given:
- 40% of staff now work remotely (post-pandemic policy change)
- Rise in insider threats (3 data breaches in past 18 months)
- Compliance requirements (NIST Cybersecurity Framework 2.0 now mandates zero-trust for critical systems)

Previous attempts to improve security included VPN enhancements (2022) and 
multi-factor authentication rollout (2024), but these were patch-based approaches 
that didn't address the fundamental perimeter model weakness.
```

### Considerations
3-5 bullet points analyzing key factors
- Pros and cons of each option
- Trade-offs and dependencies
- Stakeholder perspectives
- Resource implications

**Example:**
```
**Benefits of zero-trust:**
- Significantly reduces insider threat risk (estimated 70% reduction based on peer governments)
- Improves compliance posture (meets NIST framework requirements)
- Better supports remote/hybrid work culture
- Aligns with modern security best practices

**Challenges:**
- High upfront cost: $8-12M over 3 years
- Change management burden (staff retraining, process changes)
- Potential productivity dips during implementation
- Requires sustained funding and expertise (ongoing operational cost)

**Stakeholder views:**
- IT Security team: Strongly supports (risk mitigation)
- User community: Concerns about usability/friction
- Finance: Concerned about cost and sustainability
- Legal: Sees compliance benefits
```

### Risks
2-3 bullet points highlighting key risks
- What could go wrong?
- Mitigations in place?
- Consequences of inaction?

**Example:**
```
**Risk: Implementation overruns delay deployment** 
- Mitigation: Phased rollout (pilot → department → organization), experienced vendor partner
- Consequence: Extended window of vulnerability; missed compliance deadline

**Risk: User adoption resistance slows benefits realization**
- Mitigation: Early communication, training program, gradual access policy tightening
- Consequence: Benefits take longer to realize; continued security incidents

**Risk: Inaction leads to security breach affecting citizen data**
- Consequence: Regulatory fines, reputational damage, public trust erosion
```

### Recommendation
1-2 sentences with clear decision/action
- What should be decided?
- By when?
- Who implements?

**Example:**
```
Recommend approving zero-trust security architecture adoption with phased 
implementation over 3 years. Decision needed by June 30, 2026. IT Security 
will lead with Finance and HR support.
```

## Format Standards
- **Length**: 1-2 pages (single-spaced)
- **Tone**: Neutral, evidence-based, jargon-minimized
- **Audience**: Busy executives with limited technical background
- **Visuals**: Optional 1-page summary with decision matrix

## Quality Checklist
- ✅ Issue is crystal clear (1-2 sentences max)
- ✅ Background provides sufficient context
- ✅ Considerations are balanced and evidence-based
- ✅ Risks are realistic and mitigations are credible
- ✅ Recommendation is clear and actionable
- ✅ No jargon or technical acronyms without explanation
- ✅ All claims supported by data or reference
- ✅ Decision timeline and owner are explicit
